How we prototype?

A prototype is an early sample, model, or release of a product built to test a concept or process or to act as a thing to be replicated or learned from. - wikipedia

We have a speaker which plays songs while we work. The problem is, everytime if I wanted to play a song of my choice, I had to connect it to my system. Soon, we realized that we actually stumbled upon a problem and then we started building a prototype. The goal of this prototype [github repo] is to play songs on the server.

Basically, a prototype serves as a proof of concept. Now that we have a prototype playing songs on the server, we are thinking of extending its functionality to upvoting songs in the playlist, uploading songs on the server and many more.

We realized the importance of prototype and we want to share our ways of prototyping a concept.

First 10% and Final 10%

Your prototype builds its future on how serious you were in initial phase and final phase.

This is the optimal time when you 'think of your goal (small one preferably)', brainstorm and finally, add a finishing touch.

  • Research, research as much as you can.
  • Find an implemented module. Do not even think of implementing everything on your own.
  • Better, find out how community is implementing? (e.g. google groups)
  • Github repo, stars, changelog,
  • We need to be clever enough to utilise community experience.

So for our prototype, we wanted a node module which played songs on the command line. We got lucky and found an amazing package 'player' that served our purpose. 

npm install player.

If you go through the github page of this player (repo), you can see it has a good number of stars, forks and its recently upated too. It encouraged me to use this module.

Bake it, Wrap it and Serve it

Once you get hold of the modules, you need to sort out functionalities, wrap them in your own module. Finally, create a module on the basis of functionalities. This module should give flexibility while you code.

Player module's dissecion

  • new Player(list)
  • player.on('playing', cb)
  • player.on('playend', cb)
  • player.add(src)
  • player.play()
  • player.stop()
So, our wrapper for this is: 
    
    function BMSPlayer(playlist) {
      this.playlist = playlist;
      this.init();
    }

    BMSPlayer.prototype = {
      init : function() {
        this.player = new Player(this.playlist)
      },

      play : function() {}
      },

      ........
    }

Start with your Trump Card

The thing is, 'It is a prototype'. So code accordingly. The aim is to get prototype functional as soon as you can.

It is always a wise decision to go with what you know the best.

We chose, nodejs just for the sake of simplicity.

Think about the APIs

APIs define functionality of your client.

For our project, we wanted all clients connected to the server to get list of songs, current songs being played and so on. Our express route,

    
      /             (render html)
      /playlist     (list of songs and state)
      /upvote/:id   (upvote a song)

Utilities & co.

You should have your utility functions, like making an xmlhttprequest, a basic express server code, etc. ready with you.

More importantly, Smile :)

It's your hack, not a misery.

These are our overall process, something we feel, can help other 'wanderers' like us.
